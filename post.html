How Much Does It Cost to Build a Mobile App MVP?

8 min read
Share

To make your finance app successful in 2020, you’re going to need to include features that your users want, and also think about how to attract them to your product and keep them loyal for the long term. Here’s what we think you need to focus on:

Tight Security

Cybercriminals look at finance apps in the same way that bears look at honeycomb. They’ll stop at nothing to bypass security and steal users’ private information. Data breaches are doubly bad because criminals don’t just steal your customers’ data and money, they also steal your reputation, which is the hardest thing to recover. No one will want to use your app if you can’t give your customers a cast-iron guarantee that their cash and data will both be safe. Meeting the minimum legal standards required for data security is fine, but if you really want to inspire confidence in your product then it’s worth outsourcing your app security to experts in the field. Cybersecurity is a constantly evolving field, so it makes sense to entrust it to industry specialists. They can add features like:

  • asking for password again to update personal details

  • only showing the last few digits of card numbers

  • limiting the number of login attempts

  • location tracking to stop fraudulent transactions on your card in other countries

Two other big areas to cover are KYC (know your customer) and AML (anti-money-laundering). A ‘knowing your customer’ policy should keep fraudsters out without hurting your conversion rates. It’s important to have one because if your app makes it so easy to sign up then you must make sure that only the right people do. That’s why you need a KYC procedure at signup to ensure that you aren’t potentially opening the door to criminals, particularly because criminals will always look to exploit weaknesses in the defences of industry start-ups. KYC can include AI-driven identity checks, proofs of address, ‘liveness’ checks to make sure customers are who they say they are using photos, video, or live streaming, and more.

Anti-money-laundering procedures are needed to ensure that people of concern who may be involved in drug-trafficking, terrorism, or other illegal activities don’t get access to financial institutions to launder their funds. Many financial authorities around the world require institutions to demonstrate that they are able to monitor transactions, respond to suspicious activity and pass on their concerns to the authorities.
